subtitle

 In recent years, early detection technologies based on the analysis of miRNA expression patterns have emerged for various conditions, including cancer. However, the miRNAs analyzed in these methods are limited to those protected from RNase by proteins or lipids, leaving unprotected miRNAs under-researched. Our developed device, the “Nano Pill Bug,” focuses on protecting unprotected miRNAs from RNase. Inspired by pill bugs, this device captures miRNA and curls up to safeguard it.
 The structure consists of two DNA origami sheets and several stopper DNA strands that connect them. Each sheet has a two-layered structure, which bends due to differences in the number of base pairs, forming a cylindrical shape. These two bent sheets are secured by pre-positioned stopper DNA, balancing the tension between them to maintain a flat structure. When the stopper DNA is replaced by miRNA through strand displacement, the tension in the sheets is released, and the miRNA is encapsulated inside the cylindrical structure.
 By extending the functionality of this structure to also target small molecules like DNA or proteins, a wide range of applications can be anticipated.
